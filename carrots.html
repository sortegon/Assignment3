Easy to grow and horizontally space efficient. Must be watered, especially through droughts.

When to plant
August - March
Planting guidelines
Requires deep flower bed
Soil must be well drained, loose, and free of debris that might cause the carrot to try to grow around it
Depends on moisture
Prefers full sunlight (8 hours)
Rows 10” wide
Spacing 1-3” apart based on type of carrot planted, may need to be thinned out after seedlings are an inch tall
When to fertilize
Mix 10-10-10 fertilizer in top 3-4 inches of soil before planting
When tops are 4” tall use 2 tablespoons per 10 ft row, rake through on top of soil, don’t mix it in
If at 6-8” tall tops the leaf’s are pale, repeat previous step
Diseases/Pests to look out for
Nematodes
Alternaria leaf spot
When to harvest
70-120 days depending on type
Tips
Plant new seeds every few weeks so you have a harvest through the whole season
Once harvested, the soil is already ready to have something else planted
Slow to germinate
Inconsistent watering causes the carrot to crack but letting the foliage stay wet too long can lead to disease
Late harvests lead to tough, inedible carrots
